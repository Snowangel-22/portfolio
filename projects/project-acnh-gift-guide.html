ACNH Gift Guide

A gift-planning tool for Animal Crossing: New Horizons players

Back to Projects

Project Overview

In Animal Crossing: New Horizons, gifting is one of the primary mechanics for raising friendship with villagers. Each villager has two preferred clothing styles and two preferred colors, and the in-game catalog spans over 1,400 items. Finding the right gift means cross-referencing a wiki against your personal wardrobe — a tedious enough process that most players skip it entirely.

ACNH Gift Guide removes that friction. Players build a personal clothing inventory and pin the villagers living on their island; the app instantly surfaces which items match each villager's preferences, turning a multi-step lookup into a single glance.

Process

Three questions shaped the product direction:

  • What does the player already know vs. what do they need to look up?
  • How much setup friction is acceptable for a casual, low-stakes gaming task?
  • Can the app work without accounts, a backend, or any onboarding?

The key insight was that players know their own wardrobe but don't know the villager preference data. The app should encode all villager and item data statically, and let players manage only their inventory — keeping the experience fast and personal without any infrastructure.

User Segment

AC:NH players who actively use the gifting mechanic to raise friendship levels. They're motivated enough to collect items and interact with villagers daily, but find the lookup process — switching between a wiki and their in-game storage — tedious enough to skip.

Pain Points

  • Too much data: 391 villagers × 1,458 clothing items means an overwhelming number of combinations
  • No personal context: external wikis list all items, not just the ones you own
  • Repeated friction: the lookup has to happen fresh for every villager, every time

The core pain point was the gap between "items I already own" and "items that will actually work" — a filter problem, not a discovery problem. Players don't need to find new items; they need to know which of their existing items to use.

Solution

  • Villagers tab: pin up to 10 island residents; each card shows matched gifts from your inventory with a count badge, favorite styles as color-coded pills, and favorite colors as swatches
  • Inventory tab: two-panel layout — full 1,458-item catalog with style and source filters on the left, your current inventory in a sticky sidebar on the right; one click adds or removes any item
  • Fuzzy search via Fuse.js across item names, styles, and sources; multi-select filters for villager personality and species
  • All state persists in localStorage — no login, no backend, no data loss between sessions

Technologies Used
