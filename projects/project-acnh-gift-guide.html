ACNH Gift Guide

A gift-planning tool for Animal Crossing: New Horizons players

Back to Projects

Project Overview

In Animal Crossing: New Horizons, gifting is one of the primary mechanics for raising friendship with villagers. Each villager has two preferred clothing styles and two preferred colors, and the in-game catalog spans over 1,400 items. When I gift villagers, often they will give something back that I do not want to keep. So instead of selling them, I want to gift them to another villager who will appreciate it (more than I do).

I built this Gift Guide to remove that friction of not knowing who would like my gifts. I can log my clothing inventory, pin the villagers on my island, and the app instantly shows me which items I already own that match each villager's preferences. This replaces the multiple phone and computer tabs I use to find this information.

Process

I know my own wardrobe but I don't know the villager preference data off the top of my head. So I encoded all villager and item data statically into the app and let the user manage only their inventory — keeping the experience lightweight with no accounts or backend needed.

User Segment

AC:NH players who actively use the gifting mechanic to raise friendship levels. They're motivated enough to collect items and interact with villagers daily, but find the lookup process — switching between a wiki and their in-game storage — tedious enough to skip.

Pain Points

  • Too much data: 391 villagers × 1,458 clothing items means an overwhelming number of combinations
  • No personal context: external wikis list all items, not just the ones you own
  • Repeated friction: the lookup has to happen fresh for every villager, every time

I identified the core pain point as the gap between "items I already own" and "items that will actually work" — a filter problem, not a discovery problem. I didn't need to find new items; I needed to know which of my existing items to use.

Solution

  • Villagers tab: I can pin up to 10 island residents; each card shows matched gifts from my inventory with a count badge, favorite styles as color-coded pills, and favorite colors as swatches
  • Inventory tab: two-panel layout — full 1,458-item catalog with style and source filters on the left, my current inventory in a sticky sidebar on the right; one click adds or removes any item
  • Fuzzy search via Fuse.js across item names, styles, and sources; multi-select filters for villager personality and species
  • All state persists in localStorage — no login, no backend, no data loss between sessions

Technologies Used

React 18 Vite 5 Fuse.js
